Bisect allows users to test program state against expected values. Tests results are used as bisection points to narrow-down towards the root-cause of the underlying issue.

Customer engineering, Software Test Teams and New developers can integrate bisection trees with continuous integration to quickly classify and narrow-down the source of the problem and gain better system understanding in the process.

Module owners employ their grasp of system architecture to construct a decision tree by arranging bisection point that first test for anomalous behavior at the system level and later narrow down to module level root-causes.

One of the fundamental problems in programming is the transfer of program design across individuals. The other problem is the ability to correct express these concepts to other individuals. Bisect aims to address both of these issues.

A true understanding of the underpinnings of the nature of things comes from probing and testing for hypotheses.

The nature of Programming consists of building functional abstractions. The correctness in functionality is verifiable when tested against requirement of functionality.

One of the hardest problems in transferring understanding is to guard against misconceptions.
